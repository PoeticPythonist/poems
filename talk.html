(2) Introduction

(3) The general idea of this paper is to use a patern discovery method in order to find interesting patterns, not just patterns.

A way to mining these interesting patterns among others is via finding which of these are closed and significant. Studing music in a corpus with musical pieces, we define patterns in 2 levels: the Intra-opus patterns level and the Inter-Opus patterns level.

(4) Intra-opus patterns are the patterns that are repeated within single pieces, AND Inter-opus patterns are the patterns that occurring in different pieces.

(5) Intra-opus pattern discovery helps music analysis because it extracts the structure of a piece. Intra-opus pattern discovery helps music generation because gives us the ability to use the extructed structure in order to use it as a template to the new generated music material.

(6) Conklin's method is used to extract intra-opus pattern of a corpus that contain pieces of church music of Mozarabic rite.

As you can see, this musical notation is similar to Gregorian chant and Palaio-Byzantine music (from my point of view). This notation is characterised as pitch-unreadable or "adiastematic", because the melodic information that the neumes carries is not clear. We do not know the exact interval size that the neumes indicate. We know only the abstract contour information.

(7) Specifically, this kind of information can be capture via a categorical feature that defines 5 different values to 5 different characters: h, l, e, b, p, and o.

h indicates that this note is higher than the previous one. l indicates that this note is lower than the previous one. e indicates that this note has the same pitch to the previous one. b indicates that this note is higher or equal than the previous one. p indicates that this note is lower or equal than the previous one. AND finally, o indicates that this neume expresses an note with unclear relational pitch information. So, we do not know if the melody goes up, or down or stay to the same note.

(8) In this notation we can find sequences of neumes that for sake of simplicity we can say that works like inter-opus patterns. Theses patterns are called Formulae. Mind that formulae is a notational characteristic.

There is a consesus about the indicated music of the formulae. Researchers suggest that a formulae indicates the same melodic information in all occurences. So, must be trancribe in the same way, creating intra-opus patterns.

(9) Until Conklin and Maessen paper published in 2019, the Intra-opus patterns are defined manually, by hand.

This paper tries to tackle the problem of automatic extraction of Intra-opus patterns.

(10) This paper develops an intra-opus pattern discovery method AND reports on the precision and recall achieved by this method on a corpus of 22 annotated chant templates.
